Women, Youth and Girl Child Initiative Programme-Uganda (WAGIPU)

Empowering Vulnerable Women and Girls

Vision

To empower vulnerable women and girl children through skills development for the sustenance of themselves and their families.

Mission

To reduce inequality and stigma among vulnerable women and girls.

Brief Background

In the past, people had access to free local materials for construction and handicrafts. However, these resources are now limited, and individuals must purchase them. Traditionally, education in indigenous technologies was passed down through generations.

Poverty levels among women, girl children, and disabled females are increasing due to the lack of education for girls. Educating a girl child means educating an entire nation.

Categories of Children to be Trained

  • Secondary dropouts
  • Primary dropouts
  • Mature girls who never went to school
  • Mothers in families
  • Disabled

Courses to be Taught

  • Carpentry
  • Knitting
  • Tailoring
  • Shoemaking
  • Hairdressing
  • Baking
  • Literacy (learning to read and write)
  • Crafts
